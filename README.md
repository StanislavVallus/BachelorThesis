# Bachelor's Thesis: Equality Sets of Homomorphisms (Post Correspondence Problem)
### Mark: Excellent
#### Overview
This thesis explores the intersection of abstract algebra and theoretical computer science by investigating the algebraic formulation of the Post Correspondence Problem (PCP). Specifically, it focuses on solutions to PCP expressed via homomorphisms on free monoids, which define equality sets. 

#### Key Focus Areas & Contributions
**Language Theory & Closure Properties:** Establishes the algebraic foundations of equality sets, deriving their fundamental closure properties and mapping their relationship to the Chomsky hierarchy of languages.

**Regularity & Automata:** Defines a deterministic incomplete automaton capable of accepting equality sets. Furthermore, it identifies and analyzes special classes of homomorphisms for which the resulting equality set is guaranteed to be a regular language.

**Decidability & Complexity:** Investigates the algorithmic solvability of problems related to equality sets. This includes analyzing the computational complexity of specific instances and determining the boundaries of decidability for these algebraic structures.

*Note*: The theoretical framework of this thesis synthesizes and builds upon the foundational research of A. Salomaa, T. Harju, and J. Karhumäki.
